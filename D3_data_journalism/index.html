Health Risks & Poverty in the US

Correlations Discovered Between Smoking and Poverty

About the dataset: This dataset is based on 2014 ACS 1-year estimates from the US Census Bureau. 'Poverty (%)' is the rate of poverty in the state. 'Smoking (%)' is the rate of smoking.

Correlations/Observations: There is a positive correlation between poverty rate and smoking rates, where greater rates of poverty are associated with higher rates of smoking in the population.

Conclusions: The relationship between poverty rates and smoking rates observed here indicates that high rates of poverty are significantly associated with higher rates of smoking in the population. This also suggests a potentially causal relationship, where poverty can be a risk factor for smoking.
